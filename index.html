The ICC Cricket World Cup: A Brief History

Origins and Inception:
The concept of a Cricket World Cup was proposed by Sir Don Bradman in the 1930s.
The inaugural tournament was organized by the International Cricket Conference (now ICC) and took place in 1975.

Inaugural Tournament (1975):
Held in England, it featured eight teams.
Matches were 60 overs per side with white clothing and red balls.
The West Indies emerged as the first champions.

Development and Milestones:
The World Cup became a quadrennial event, with formats evolving to include more teams.
India's 1983 win and the introduction of colored clothing in 1992 were key milestones.
The 1996 tournament, hosted by the subcontinent, showcased cricket's passion.

Continued Success: The ICC Cricket World Cup remains a premier cricket event.
It has contributed to the sport's global growth and the emergence of cricketing legends.

The Cricket World Cup's rich history and evolution have made it a globally celebrated sporting spectacle, shaping the future of cricket.
